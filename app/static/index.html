About GhostNote

GhostNote was built around a simple idea: if the server never receives your secret in readable form, it can never leak it. Your secret is encrypted with AES-256-GCM directly in your browser. The decryption key is embedded in the share link's URL fragment (#key) — a part of the URL that browsers never transmit to servers, by the rules of the web itself.

When the recipient opens the link, the server atomically deletes the ciphertext in the same operation that returns it. After that, nothing remains — not in the database, not in logs, not in memory. Even if you wanted to recover it, you couldn't.

GhostNote is fully open source. Every line of code that handles your data — especially crypto.js — is publicly readable. You don't have to trust us. You can verify.

How Your Secret Stays Secret

Encrypted In Your Browser

AES-256-GCM encryption runs on your device before anything is sent. We only ever receive unreadable ciphertext.

Key Never Reaches Us

Your decryption key lives only in the link's #fragment. Browsers never send fragments to servers — this is enforced by RFC 3986, not just our word.

Destroyed on First View

Reading a secret atomically deletes it via MongoDB's find_one_and_delete(). Even we can't retrieve it — there's nothing left to retrieve.

Auto-Expiry

A MongoDB TTL index auto-deletes unrevealed secrets after your chosen time. No cron jobs, no manual cleanup — the database enforces it natively.
